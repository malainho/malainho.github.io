Do angels and demons exist?

This page is step 17 of a path built in order — the conclusions of this theme rest on the previous ones. You may read this page, but the full journey begins with the first question.

Start the path from the beginning →

Two figures run through all traditions. Tom explains what each really is — and why one of them cannot exist at all.

— What are angels?

— Angels are more evolved spirits. It could not be otherwise. If God created angels different from spirits, He would be being unjust, because spirits would only progress through their own effort, while angels would have nothing to endure. That idea annuls the principle of God's supreme justice and goodness.

— Yes, it makes complete sense that it should be so. And demons, devils, what are they?

— There are no beings eternally turned towards evil; they are merely a figure representing wickedness. If spirits continually perfect themselves, there is no room left for the existence of demons, of devils, or of any other name one might wish to give them.

— Yes, reincarnation makes all spirits good.

— Yes, there is no escaping evolution. Sooner or later, spirits become pure.

Excerpt from the work The Great Discovery, by César Manuel Malainho de Oliveira.
